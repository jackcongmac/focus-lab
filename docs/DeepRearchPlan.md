# FocusLab – Deep Research Plan

## 1. Purpose

Validate whether FocusLab can genuinely support children’s development in focus, attention, and task completion.

This research will define:
- What benefits are realistic
- What claims are safe and credible
- What training patterns are appropriate
- What parents are willing to trust and pay for

---

## 2. Core Questions

### 2.1 Effectiveness

- Can short, repeated digital exercises improve:
  - attention span
  - visual recognition
  - following instructions
  - task completion

- Under what conditions:
  - session duration
  - frequency (per day / week)
  - total training period (weeks)

---

### 2.2 Training Dose

- What is the recommended:
  - duration per session (3 / 5 / 10+ min)
  - sessions per day
  - sessions per week

- What patterns are supported:
  - short frequent sessions vs longer sessions
  - single-theme vs mixed-theme training

---

### 2.3 Target Outcome

Define what FocusLab actually improves:

Possible candidates:
- sustained attention
- selective attention
- instruction following
- visual discrimination
- task persistence

Must clarify:
- what we can realistically claim
- what we should NOT claim (e.g. therapy / treatment)

---

### 2.4 Parent Perception & Value

- What makes parents believe a product is valuable?
- What signals "this is helping my child"?
- What causes distrust?

Key factors:
- visible structure (routine)
- measurable outcome (summary)
- sense of progress
- control over usage

---

### 2.5 Willingness to Pay

- What triggers subscription decisions:
  - perceived improvement
  - routine formation
  - daily habit
  - professional credibility

- What is required before payment:
  - trial period
  - visible progress
  - trust in system

---

## 3. Research Areas

### 3.1 Scientific / Clinical Literature

Focus areas:
- executive function training in children
- attention training (non-clinical and ADHD)
- early childhood cognitive development
- digital interventions for learning

Key variables to extract:
- duration per session
- frequency
- total program length
- outcome measures
- effect size (if available)

---

### 3.2 Existing Products

Analyze:
- EndeavorRx (FDA-cleared)
- educational apps (Khan Kids, etc.)
- therapy-adjacent apps

Look for:
- session structure
- duration
- progression logic
- parent communication
- claims vs evidence

---

### 3.3 Parent Interviews (Critical)

Interview 5–10 parents:

Questions:
- What do you want to improve in your child?
- How long are you willing to let them practice?
- What makes you believe an app is “helpful”?
- What would make you pay for it?
- What makes you distrust an app?

---

### 3.4 Internal Product Data (Later Stage)

Once prototype is used:

Track:
- session duration
- completion rate
- error rate
- session frequency
- drop-off points

---

## 4. Hypotheses (To Validate)

H1:
Short, consistent sessions are more sustainable than long sessions.

H2:
Parents prefer time-based routines over level-based progress.

H3:
Parents value measurable outcomes more than entertainment.

H4:
Perceived improvement is driven by:
- completion
- reduced errors
- consistency

---

## 5. Claim Boundaries

Until validated, FocusLab should NOT claim:

- therapy
- clinical treatment
- ADHD improvement
- medical effectiveness

Safe positioning:

"Guided practice for focus, attention, and following instructions"

---

## 6. Output of This Research

This research must produce:

1. Recommended session model:
   - duration
   - frequency
   - progression

2. Clear value statement:
   - what FocusLab helps improve

3. Parent-facing language:
   - how results are communicated

4. Product constraints:
   - what features are required
   - what can be deferred

---

## 7. Success Criteria

We can move forward only if:

- We understand realistic benefit scope
- We define safe and credible product claims
- We identify what parents trust and value
- We align product design with research findings

---

## 8. Key Principle

Do not design features before understanding value.

Value → System → Feature
NOT
Feature → Assume Value
